# NL-002 References

## Signal Processing Foundations

1. **Oppenheim, A.V. & Schafer, R.W. (2010).** *Discrete-Time Signal Processing.* 3rd ed. Pearson.
   - The standard DSP reference. Chapters 1-7 cover the foundations used in this module.

2. **Widrow, B. & Stearns, S.D. (1985).** *Adaptive Signal Processing.* Prentice-Hall.
   - Adaptive filtering theory relevant to adaptive artifact removal.

3. **Mallat, S. (2009).** *A Wavelet Tour of Signal Processing.* 3rd ed. Academic Press.
   - Comprehensive wavelet theory for time-frequency analysis.

## Neural Signal Processing

4. **Nunez, P.L. & Srinivasan, R. (2006).** *Electric Fields of the Brain.* Oxford University Press.
   - The physics of neural signal generation, filtering by biological tissue.

5. **Freeman, W.J. (1975).** *Mass Action in the Nervous System.* Academic Press.
   - Neural mass models that explain the spectral properties of neural signals.

6. **Quiroga, R.Q. et al. (2000).** "Neuroscience applications of independent component analysis." *J. Neuroscience Methods*, 111(2), 111-131.
   - ICA for artifact removal in neural recordings.

## Artifact Detection and Removal

7. **Jung, T.P. et al. (2000).** "Removing Electroencephalographic Artifacts by Blind Source Separation." *Psychophysiology*, 37(2), 163-178.
   - The foundational ICA-based EEG artifact removal paper.

8. **Urigüen, J.A. & Garcia-Zapirain, B. (2015).** "EEG artifact removal — state-of-the-art and guidelines." *J. Neural Engineering*, 12(3).
   - Comprehensive review of EEG artifact removal methods.

## Feature Extraction

9. **Hjorth, B. (1970).** "EEG analysis based on time domain properties." *Electroencephalography and Clinical Neurophysiology*, 29(3), 306-310.
   - Original Hjorth parameters paper.

10. **Burioka, N. et al. (2005).** "Approximate entropy and spectral entropy." * Psychiatry and Clinical Neurosciences*, 59(5), 537-541.
    - Spectral entropy for neural signal characterization.

## Time-Frequency Analysis

11. **Cohen, L. (1995).** *Time-Frequency Analysis.* Prentice-Hall.
    - Comprehensive reference on STFT, wavelets, and other time-frequency methods.

12. **Bruns, A. (2004).** "Fourier-, Hilbert- and wavelet-based signal analysis." *Brain Topography*, 16(4), 277-286.
    - Practical comparison of time-frequency methods for neural signals.

## Adversarial ML (from NL-001, relevant here for feature-level attacks)

13. **Zhang, H. et al. (2019).** "Adversarial Attacks on EEG-Based BCIs." *IEEE TNSRE*.
14. **Majumdar, A. et al. (2020).** "Adversarial Robustness of Neural Signal Classifiers." *J. Neural Engineering*.

## Digital Filtering Security

15. **Chen, S. et al. (2020).** "Robustness of neural network-based EEG decoding against adversarial attacks." *IEEE TNSRE*.
16. **Saeedi, S. et al. (2023).** "Adversarial attacks on deep learning-based EEG classification systems." *Frontiers in Neuroscience*.

## Compression

17. **Abdoli, S. et al. (2022).** "Lossy compression of neural signals for wireless implantable devices." *IEEE TBME*.
18. **Harrison, M.T. et al. (2021).** "Sparse neural signal compression for wireless brain-computer interfaces." *IEEE TNSRE*.

## Spike Processing

19. **Quiroga, R.Q. (2012).** "Spike sorting." *Current Opinion in Neurobiology*, 22(4), 557-564.
20. **Rossant, C. et al. (2016).** "Spike sorting for large, high-density neuronal recordings." *Nature Neuroscience*, 19, 634-641.

## Standards

21. **IEC 60601-2-26:** Medical electrical equipment — Part 2-26: Particular requirements for EEG.
22. **FDA Guidance:** "Clinical EEG" — recommendations for clinical EEG recording parameters.
