# Validation: EEG Signal Generation

**Audience**: Academic Researchers, Data Scientists

## Purpose
This document bounds the validity of the EEG signals generated by VIREON during simulations.

## What is Simulated
VIREON currently uses procedural noise generation mixed with discrete synthetic wave packets (e.g., N400 Event-Related Potentials) to simulate neural activity.

## Equations & Assumptions
The simulated EEG is constructed using additive synthesis:
```python
EEG(t) = Baseline_Pink_Noise + alpha_waves(t) + beta_waves(t) + ERP_spikes(t)
```

**Assumptions**:
- **Linearity**: The model assumes brain states can be linearly combined.
- **Isotropic Conductivity**: The model assumes perfectly spherical volume conduction without accounting for skull thickness or cerebrospinal fluid shunting.
- **No Artifacts**: The generated signal is free of muscle artifacts (EMG) and eye blinks (EOG) unless explicitly injected by the Attack Framework.

## Limitations & Out of Scope
- **Not Medically Validated**: The EEG data is completely synthetic. An anomaly detection system trained purely on VIREON EEG data will likely fail catastrophically on real human data.
- **Lack of Non-Stationarity**: Real EEG is highly non-stationary. Our pink-noise baseline maintains statistical stationarity over the duration of short simulations.

## Future Work
We intend to replace the procedural generation engine with a playback engine capable of streaming real, pre-recorded clinical datasets (e.g., from OpenBCI or Temple University Hospital EEG Corpus).
