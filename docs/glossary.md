# VIREON Glossary & Formal Definitions

VIREON operates at the intersection of neuroscience, cybersecurity, and control theory. To maintain academic and engineering rigor, the following terms are formally defined within the scope of this repository.

> [!NOTE]
> VIREON is an **evolving research platform and reference implementation**. The phenomena described below are theoretical threat models designed for simulation and mitigation research, aligned with established cybersecurity and clinical safety standards.

## Clinical and Physiological Attacks

### Neural Ransomware / Unauthorized Stimulation
**Formal Definition:** An attack vector where an adversary gains unauthorized write-access to an active neurostimulation implant and modifies the stimulation parameters beyond clinically safe limits.
**Mathematical Bounds:** Modeled as an override of `stimulation_amplitude_ma` where demanded current exceeds safety threshold (e.g. 4.0mA).

### Unauthorized Cognitive State Inference
**Formal Definition:** The interception and classification of raw electrophysiological telemetry (e.g., EEG) by an unauthorized third party to infer sensitive states.
**Context:** Implies compromise of data confidentiality boundary at the BLE link or cloud endpoint.

### Bifurcation Forcing / Pathological Synchronization
**Formal Definition:** An advanced closed-loop attack where the adversary manipulates the sensed biomarker to force the controller into a positive feedback loop.

## Platform and Cybersecurity Terms

1. **SecurityEngine**: The heuristic threat intelligence module that evaluates anomalies.
2. **ZTA (Zero-Trust Architecture)**: A security framework requiring explicit contextual trust for every clinical action.
3. **SPDF (Secure Product Development Framework)**: The FDA-mandated cybersecurity lifecycle framework.
4. **CPIF (Cyber-Physical Interface Framework)**: The theoretical boundary where digital telemetry converts into physical actuation.
5. **NISS (Neuro-Intrusion Scoring System)**: A metric for quantifying the severity of a cyber-physical attack.
6. **qTARA**: Quantitative Threat Assessment and Remediation Analysis.
7. **STRIDE**: Spoofing, Tampering, Repudiation, Information Disclosure, Denial of Service, Elevation of Privilege.
8. **CWE (Common Weakness Enumeration)**: A community-developed list of software and hardware weakness types.
9. **MITRE ATT&CK**: A globally-accessible knowledge base of adversary tactics.
10. **Digital Twin**: A real-time, stateful representation of the clinical device.
11. **LFP (Local Field Potential)**: Aggregate electrophysiological signal generated by neurons, commonly sensed by DBS.
12. **BCI (Brain-Computer Interface)**: A system that allows communication between the brain and an external device.
13. **DBS (Deep Brain Stimulation)**: Neurotechnology involving implants that send electrical impulses to specific brain targets.
14. **VNS (Vagus Nerve Stimulation)**: Neurotechnology delivering electrical impulses to the vagus nerve.
15. **NeuroDSL**: VIREON's embedded domain-specific language for bounded clinical therapy routines.
16. **Coordinator**: The orchestrator managing time ticks, data flow, and engine coordination.
17. **EventBus**: A pub-sub broker managing inter-module communications.
18. **ReplayEngine**: Feeds pre-recorded dataset signals into the Digital Twin.
19. **PhysicsEngine**: Handles thermodynamic and battery calculations.
20. **Brownout**: A state where the simulated battery dips below operational thresholds.
21. **FDA 524B**: Mandate for medical device cybersecurity requirements.
22. **SBOM (Software Bill of Materials)**: Inventory of software components.
23. **Telemetry**: Data streamed from the simulated implant to a programmer/cloud.
24. **LSL (Lab Streaming Layer)**: A system for the unified collection of measurement time series.
25. **EEG (Electroencephalography)**: Electrophysiological monitoring of brain activity.
26. **Spoofing**: Falsifying telemetry or sensory data.
27. **DoS (Denial of Service)**: Exhausting device resources to prevent therapy.
28. **Rollback Attack**: Forcing a device to revert to an older, vulnerable firmware.
29. **Replay Attack**: Capturing and resending valid telemetry to deceive the system.
30. **E2EE (End-to-End Encryption)**: Encrypted communication link (simulated).
