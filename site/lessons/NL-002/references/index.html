    NL-002 References

    Signal Processing Foundations

    1. Oppenheim, A.V. & Schafer, R.W. (2010). Discrete-Time Signal Processing. 3rd ed. Pearson.

    2. The standard DSP reference. Chapters 1-7 cover the foundations used in this module.

    3. Widrow, B. & Stearns, S.D. (1985). Adaptive Signal Processing. Prentice-Hall.

    4. Adaptive filtering theory relevant to adaptive artifact removal.

    5. Mallat, S. (2009). A Wavelet Tour of Signal Processing. 3rd ed. Academic Press.

    6. Comprehensive wavelet theory for time-frequency analysis.

    Neural Signal Processing

    1. Nunez, P.L. & Srinivasan, R. (2006). Electric Fields of the Brain. Oxford University Press.

    2. The physics of neural signal generation, filtering by biological tissue.

    3. Freeman, W.J. (1975). Mass Action in the Nervous System. Academic Press.

    4. Neural mass models that explain the spectral properties of neural signals.

    5. Quiroga, R.Q. et al. (2000). "Neuroscience applications of independent component analysis." J. Neuroscience Methods, 111(2), 111-131.

    6. ICA for artifact removal in neural recordings.

    Artifact Detection and Removal

    1. Jung, T.P. et al. (2000). "Removing Electroencephalographic Artifacts by Blind Source Separation." Psychophysiology, 37(2), 163-178.

    2. The foundational ICA-based EEG artifact removal paper.

    3. Urigüen, J.A. & Garcia-Zapirain, B. (2015). "EEG artifact removal — state-of-the-art and guidelines." J. Neural Engineering, 12(3).

    4. Comprehensive review of EEG artifact removal methods.

    Feature Extraction

    1. Hjorth, B. (1970). "EEG analysis based on time domain properties." Electroencephalography and Clinical Neurophysiology, 29(3), 306-310.

    2. Original Hjorth parameters paper.

    3. Burioka, N. et al. (2005). "Approximate entropy and spectral entropy." * Psychiatry and Clinical Neurosciences*, 59(5), 537-541.

      • Spectral entropy for neural signal characterization.

    Time-Frequency Analysis

    1. Cohen, L. (1995). Time-Frequency Analysis. Prentice-Hall.

      • Comprehensive reference on STFT, wavelets, and other time-frequency methods.

    2. Bruns, A. (2004). "Fourier-, Hilbert- and wavelet-based signal analysis." Brain Topography, 16(4), 277-286.

      • Practical comparison of time-frequency methods for neural signals.

    Adversarial ML (from NL-001, relevant here for feature-level attacks)

    1. Zhang, H. et al. (2019). "Adversarial Attacks on EEG-Based BCIs." IEEE TNSRE.
    2. Majumdar, A. et al. (2020). "Adversarial Robustness of Neural Signal Classifiers." J. Neural Engineering.

    Digital Filtering Security

    1. Chen, S. et al. (2020). "Robustness of neural network-based EEG decoding against adversarial attacks." IEEE TNSRE.
    2. Saeedi, S. et al. (2023). "Adversarial attacks on deep learning-based EEG classification systems." Frontiers in Neuroscience.

    Compression

    1. Abdoli, S. et al. (2022). "Lossy compression of neural signals for wireless implantable devices." IEEE TBME.
    2. Harrison, M.T. et al. (2021). "Sparse neural signal compression for wireless brain-computer interfaces." IEEE TNSRE.

    Spike Processing

    1. Quiroga, R.Q. (2012). "Spike sorting." Current Opinion in Neurobiology, 22(4), 557-564.
    2. Rossant, C. et al. (2016). "Spike sorting for large, high-density neuronal recordings." Nature Neuroscience, 19, 634-641.

    Standards

    1. IEC 60601-2-26: Medical electrical equipment — Part 2-26: Particular requirements for EEG.
    2. FDA Guidance: "Clinical EEG" — recommendations for clinical EEG recording parameters.
